RhaetoChat

Language Inclusion, Technology, and Resource Advancement

RhaetoChat is a data collection project focused on developing language technologies for Romansh and Ladin, two under-resourced languages of the Alpine region.

The project combines safe human-written prompts, post-edited machine translation, native-speaker review, and preference rankings over model responses so future multilingual LLMs can better serve these varieties.

Where We Work

Many varieties, little digital coverage

Rhaeto-Romance is not one uniform target. 🪜 LITRA helps organize work across Rumantsch Grischun, Sursilvan, Sutsilvan, Surmiran, Puter, Vallader, and selected Ladin varieties so translations, comments, and source flags stay tied to the exact linguistic context.

Why It Matters

Current LLMs are not reliable enough

The slide comparison shows a simple test: translating potato into several Rhaeto-Romance varieties. Most model outputs are wrong. 🪜 LITRA is designed to collect the human-reviewed data needed for better post-training, machine translation, and post-editing research.

Project Plan

From safe prompts to open datasets

RhaetoChat starts from safe prompts in the Open Assistant Conversations Dataset, translates them into Romansh and Ladin varieties, and asks native speakers to post-edit prompts and rank model responses. The resulting data is intended for supervised fine-tuning, reward-model research, machine translation, and dataset documentation.

Human review Paid native post-editors and annotators check translations and preference rankings.
Quality and ethics Only prompts marked safe are used, with manual screening before assignment.
Open release The planned dataset release targets Apache-2.0 on Hugging Face, with Zenodo backup.
Community partners Lia Rumantscha, unibz, UZH, and the University of Innsbruck connect research with language expertise.
